Current Research

Predictive Privacy: Master's Thesis

  • Developed a novel ’Predictive Privacy’ framework to quantify privacy harm and provide a concrete basis for regulators and courts to assess actual injury due to the disclosure of private information.
  • Constructed a comprehensive database with synthetic data, incorporating differential privacy techniques, to simulate the statistical distribution of sensitive attributes within the American population.
  • Implemented a semi-supervised machine learning algorithm to predict potential privacy risks across various disclosure scenarios
  • 2 paper manuscripts (law review article and technical paper) work in progress, and masters thesis almost complete
